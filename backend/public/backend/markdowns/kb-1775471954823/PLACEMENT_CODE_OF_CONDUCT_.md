## **GENERAL GUIDELINES**

1. The code of conduct applies to all students participating in campus


placements for the academic year 2026-27 at the University Institute of


Engineering and Technology (UIET) Panjab University, Chandigarh.


2. All students are required to carefully read and adhere to this code of conduct


throughout the entire placement process.


3. Placements are given by the University Institute of Engineering and


Technology to the students of B.E./M.E. under the following conditions:


a) The Code of conduct is followed by all the students.


b) No recruiter gives negative feedback about students.


c) Any student willing to participate for on-campus placements has to


compulsorily register on the TPC Student Forum and has to sign an


undertaking to be eligible for the placement process

## **PLACEMENT POLICY**

**Eligibility:**
All registered students at UIET are eligible for placement support.

**One Student One Job Policy:**
Each student is entitled to one job offer initially.

**Placement Slabs:**
Placement opportunities are divided into three slabs based on CTC:

   - **Slab A:** 3.0 – 6.0 LPA (≤ 6.0 LPA)

   - **Slab B:**    - 6.0 – < 10.0 LPA

   - **Slab C:** ≥ 10.0 LPA

**Maximum Offers:**
A student can hold a maximum of **2 job offers**, subject to TPC approval.

**Slab Movement Rule:**
A student can apply for another opportunity only if:

   - The new job is in a **different slab**, and

   - The **CTC difference is at least +3.0 LPA** (or as decided by TPC).

**Restriction for Slab C:**
Students with an offer of **≥ 10 LPA** are **not eligible for further placements** .

   - Exception: If the new offer is **at least double the current CTC**, the student
may appeal to TPC.

**Company Restrictions:**
If a company restricts further participation after selection, the student must comply.

   - Exception: TPC may allow participation if the package difference is
significantly high (e.g., >10 LPA).

**Modes of Job Offers (Subject to Slab Rules)**
_This rule is applicable only if the candidate satisfies the slab movement criteria._

   - If a student has an **FTE + Internship offer**, then he/she is eligible only for:

`o` FTE + Internship

   - If a student has **only an FTE offer**, then he/she is eligible for:

`o` Only Internship

`o` Only FTE

`o` FTE + Internship

   - If a student has **only an internship offer**, then he/she is eligible for:

`o` Only FTE

`o` FTE + Internship

**PPO Restriction:**
Students with **8th semester internships with PPO** are not eligible for further
placements.

**Latest Offer Rule:**
If a student secures a new offer, it **automatically replaces the previous one** .
**Internship Limit:**
Only **one “Intern-only” offer** is allowed.

**Offer Rejection Policy:**
Rejecting an offer after selection leads to **complete debarment** from further
placements

**PPO Counting Rule:**
A PPO is counted as **one job offer** out of the maximum allowed.

**Non-Attendance Penalty:**
If a student does not appear after applying, they may be **debarred from future drives**
(as decided by TPC).

**Exceptional Cases:**
If a student faces genuine issues (e.g., no joining date/letter), TPC may allow reparticipation.

**TPC Authority:**
TPC holds full authority to **modify rules**, and its decisions are **final and binding** .

**Policy Flexibility:**
Rules may be **added, amended, or removed at any time** by TPC.

## **PLACEMENT PROCESS**

**Registration for Participation and Regulations:**


1. Students must register within the specified timeframe to participate in

specific company recruitment drives. The registration process details
will be communicated through notifications. Once registered, students
are obligated to attend the scheduled company recruitment processes.
Failure to attend without valid reason will result in guideline violation.


2. Placement-related activities may occur beyond 10 pm, given the fast
paced nature of the process.


3. Students should be prepared for short-notice gatherings as part of the

placement process.


4. Students will receive notice before Guest Lectures, Pre-placement

Talks, or other activities. All students are expected to be seated in the
auditorium at least 15 minutes before the start of any such activity. No
late entry would be permitted.


5. Mobile phones must be turned off during Guest Lectures, PPTs, and

Seminars. Once these activities commence, students are not allowed to
leave the auditorium.


6. Students are strictly prohibited from contacting the recruiting company

or its HR representatives directly before or after the placement drive.
All communication must be routed through the Training and Placement
Cell (TPC), failure to do so will result in guideline violation.


**Dress Code:**
Students are required to adhere strictly to the prescribed dress code for all activities
organized by the Placement Cell, unless otherwise specified. The attire includes a
**white shirt** paired with **black trousers or skirts**, and **black formal shoes or bellies** .


Students must be **dressed elegantly** and presentably at all times, that is shirts should
be properly ironed, and shabby or untidy clothing will not be permitted under any
circumstances.

## **ATTENDANCE RULES**

1. Each student should maintain attendance of 100% attendance for Guest

Lectures/Seminars and Campus Engagement Sessions. Proper
attendance records will be maintained by Placement Cell.


2. Students will not be allowed to sit for the selection process of a company

if they do not attend its PPT(Pre Placement Talk) at all costs.


3. Every student even after he/she has been placed has to follow all the

attendance rules regarding the Guest Lectures and Pre-placement Talks.


4. If, after placement, a student’s attendance goes below the minimum

required criteria, then a letter will be sent by the Director to the company
where he/she has been placed and strict actions can be taken.

## **LAPSES AND CONSEQUENCES**

**Lapses include:**


1. Disobeying directives from the Placement Cell/ Placement Officer/ Director.


2. Failing to meet deadlines set by the Placement Cell/ Placement Officer/

Director.


3. Not punctually attending scheduled activities (PPT/Placement Test/Placement

Interview).


4. Violating attendance rules.


5. Engaging in any form of malpractice or nuisance during the placement

process.


6. Contacting recruiters or companies visiting UIET without prior authorization

from the Training and Placement Cell (TPC).


7. Any action disrupting the regular functioning of the placement process.


8. **Confidentiality** : Students must not disclose information about other

companies that have visited or will visit the campus to any recruiting company
at any costs.


**Consequences of Violation:**


Any student found in violation may face disciplinary action, including:


1. Being debarred from participating in company recruitment processes.


2. Suspension from the institution.

## **GENERAL OBLIGATIONS**

**1.** **Respectful Treatment:** We are obligated to treat all individuals with respect

and dignity.


**2.** Students are expected to maintain decorum at all times; any disrespectful

conduct towards the Training and Placement Cell (TPC) will be treated
seriously and may invite disciplinary action.


**3.** **Prohibited Actions:**


a) We must not engage in any behavior that could lead to a violation of

equality rights by our authority.


b) Bullying of any individual is strictly prohibited.


c) Threatening or intimidating any person who is or may be:


i) A witness, or


ii) Involved in the administration of any investigations or proceedings

regarding a member's alleged failure to comply with this Code of
Conduct, is not allowed.


iii) Threatening or intimidating a complainant is also prohibited.


d) We must refrain from any activity that could compromise the

impartiality of individuals associated with the Training and Placement
Cell.


e) Disclosure of confidential information, whether received in

confidence or acquired by us, is prohibited unless requested by a
member of the Training and Placement Cell.


**4.** Engaging in any form of malpractice like cheating or dishonest conduct

during the placement process will result in immediate debarment from all
placement activities.


**5.** Each student is required to carry two printed copies of their resume during all

placement drives.


**6.** **Institutional Integrity:** Individually, we must avoid behaviour that could

bring disrepute to the institute.

**7.** **Conflict Resolution:**


a) Departmental conflicts should be resolved within the department itself.


b) Contacting media for any purpose related to departmental conflicts is

emphasizing the importance of resolving conflicts internally and maintaining
