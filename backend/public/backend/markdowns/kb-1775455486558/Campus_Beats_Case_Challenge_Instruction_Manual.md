**Instruction Manual for Teams**


The **ZS Campus Beats Case Challenge** is designed as a comprehensive two-part evaluation that brings
together collaborative problem solving and individual capability assessment in a dynamic, real-world
business context. Participants will engage in a team-based case study that mirrors real industry
challenges, requiring structured thinking, data interpretation, and strategic decision-making. This is
followed by an individual assessment aimed at evaluating personal strengths in analytical thinking,
logical reasoning, and the effective use of AI and prompt engineering. Together, these components
create a holistic experience that not only tests technical and cognitive skills but also emphasizes
innovation, adaptability, and the ability to leverage AI-driven approaches to solve complex problems.


**Event Schedule:**


**Challenge Opens:** 11:00 AM IST


**Challenge Closes:** 08:00 PM IST


**Team Case Duration:** 120 minutes (timed once started)


**Latest Possible Start Time:** 06:00 PM IST


**Structure of the Event:**


**Part 1: Case Study Round (Team-Based)**


The challenge begins with a team-based case study designed to assess your approach to solving realworld business problems through structured analysis and strategic thinking.


Each question within the case will:


  - Be supported by a relevant dataset for interpretation


  - Require the application of prompt engineering techniques to extract insights and develop
recommendations


To encourage effective use of AI tools, a negative marking mechanism will be applied if teams bypass
prompt-based exploration and directly rely on the case content without engaging with the dataset.

**Part 2: Individual Quiz**


Following the case round, participants will complete an individual assessment that includes:


  - 7–10 situational questions focused on prompt engineering and context writing


  - Cognitive questions to evaluate logical reasoning and problem-solving ability


  - Additional sections to assess core analytical and technical capabilities


This round is designed to evaluate how effectively candidates can structure prompts, define context,
think critically, and generate meaningful outputs using AI.


Overall, the challenge is designed to evaluate **analytical thinking, problem-solving approach, and**
**the effective application of AI and prompt engineering** in solving complex business scenarios.


_Note: The hackathon is a single-attempt assessment and must be completed without interruption_
_once initiated._


**System Requirements:**


Each participant must use an individual laptop (No shared devices).


A working webcam and microphone is mandatory — the session is recorded.


Ensure a stable and high-speed internet connection throughout the event.

**Instruction Manual for Team Captain**


As the captain, you’ll lead your team through the case and be responsible for the final submission.


**Key Responsibilities:**


  - Coordinate your team to start the challenge between the allocated time window

  - Ensure all team members are present and have joined the session together.

  - Lead the discussion and ensure contributions from everyone.

  - Monitor the timer and submit the final case response within 90 minutes.

  - Only the team captain has submission access.


**System Requirements:**


Use a laptop with a functioning webcam and microphone.


A **strong and reliable internet connection** is essential for a smooth experience.
